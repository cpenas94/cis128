Progressive Web Applications

(PWA)

About PWA's:

Progressive web apps, or PWA's, are a major step forward in helping make the modern web environment more progressive, both for developers and users alike. There are a few security advantages in regards to PWA's, as well as performance enhancements.

When developing a PWA, I reccomend testing it inside of Chrome, as it's chrome://serviceworker-internals page is very useful for detailed information in regards to all service worker state's. Another chrome page that's helpful is chrome://inspect/#service-workers. It allows you to see registered service workers, and inspect them if desired. Not only does it give verbose information, it also allows some control over the service worker thru a register/unregister button and a start/stop button, as well.

One of the major advantages gained with PWA's is reliability. No matter the network state of the client, loading is instant because of it's use of service workers. The service worker ensures that all necessary resources are cached inside the client's memory, ready to answer requests whether online or offline!

Another advantage gained by developing a PWA is security. In order to qualify as a PWA, your app must use the strictest security standards including HTTPS and HTTP/2. Not only does this secure the server, it also gives customers and clients piece of mind. Studies prove this piece of mind translates into more sales and profits for the site owners.
